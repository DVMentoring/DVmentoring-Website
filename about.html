About Us

At Dieu Vivant, we are determined to create a rich learning environment with the aim of stimulating your child’s physical, social, emotional, and intellectual growth as an added value to the Education Standards recommended by the Ghana Education System. Each child should be receiving and provided with experiences that enhance confidence and spark their imagination, which is essential in the growing stages of child development regardless of which standards they should follow. This approach ensures that each child’s learning experience will be nurtured at the proper pace and will give them a strong educational foundation for a bright future.

Our goal is to continuously push the boundaries to build a strong foundation for growth by providing developmentally appropriate add-ons to the National Curriculum so that each year, what the kids are learning goes beyond what is prescribed. We are a school that likes to be flexibility and try new things and that is why at any given moment you show up to the school, you might find guest staff, guest students from abroad sitting in on classes or participating in projects, or giving students the freedom to experiment.

We have both a domestic and an international group of advisors deep rooted in education research and childhood development and have started focusing a lot of our learning programs on collaborative learning experiences.

This school was built on the aspirations of its founder Mrs. Comfort Akua Addai-Mensah. At first it started because she realized parents didn’t have anywhere to leave their kids to go to work or the market place and so it was to be a simple very affordable day care. After a while, as she realized the difference it was making in parents lives, and the growth she was seeing in the kids who otherwise wouldn’t be going to school, she decided there has to be something more. It had to go beyond helping parents and making an impact in the kids lives, it had to be a total transformational place which should have a lasting impact in the community and the people who live in that community. But to do that, it would have to come at a cost so without hesitation, she started saving up all her money from her career in health care with one aim: to build a transformational institution. Till this day, if you ask her why she built the school, she modestly says “ To be surrounded by kids in her retirement so she can witness their growth and transformation on a daily basis with the hope that they in turn can help transform their community”. It is on this basis that the school exist, constantly thrive to exceed, and will continue to exist as an institution. Every student, every staff member that walks on its’ grounds will be indoctrinated on the principles and foundation she has laid out: A transformational mindset – otherwise there is no reason to be part of the program whether you are a student or a staff member. It is our sole and most important reason to expel a member. Not because you are not a good student, not because you can’t afford it, not because you were misbehaving. Simply because you stopped to or didn’t want to maintain a transformational mindset. Without compromising our principles, our foundation continuously adjust and innovate. An environment of nimbleness not just in the operations of the school but the mindset amongst the students that walk through its grounds as they look to the future.
